"The certification and licensing section of a healthcare resume carries more weight than in virtually any other industry. Healthcare is regulated at federal, state, and institutional levels, and employers face significant legal liability if they hire someone whose credentials are not current or valid for the jurisdiction in which they practice. This section must be precise, complete, and easy to verify.\n"
"\n"
"Organize your certifications in a clear, scannable format. Lead with your primary professional license, such as your RN, LPN, MD, DO, PA, or therapy license. Include the full license title, the issuing state board, your license number if appropriate for your region, and the expiration date. If you hold licenses in multiple states or participate in the Nurse Licensure Compact, note this explicitly as multi-state licensure significantly increases your value to healthcare systems operating across state lines.\n"
"\n"
"Below your primary license, list specialty certifications in order of relevance to the position you are targeting. Group life support certifications together (BLS, ACLS, PALS, NRP) and then list specialty certifications (CCRN, CEN, OCN, CNOR, and similar). For each certification, include the certifying body and the expiration date. If you have recently passed a certification exam and are awaiting the official credential, you may note it as pending with the expected date.\n"
